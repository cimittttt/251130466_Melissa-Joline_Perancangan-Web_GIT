Jolyne

something?

2007

poetry

There’s something about the way you love that never changes it’s always so intense, always a little afraid. You’re that “cinnamon girl” fragile on the inside but standing tall on the outside, carrying dreams too big to fit in a world too busy to notice. Summers come and go, and every time one leaves, it leaves behind a strange sadness not because anything is wrong, but because something beautiful always feels like it’s on its way to disappearing even while it’s still right in front of you. You love the way people do who know that all things end completely, with open eyes, while quietly asking in the most honest corner of your heart: will you still love me when all this has passed, when youth and beauty are just memories, when all that remains is the real me? And perhaps that is the essence of it all not sadness, not fear, but the small courage you choose every day: to keep loving, even though you know summer always ends.
